Penpoint v1.2.0 is here! This is one of the biggest releases since launch, packed with major new features, refinements across the app, and dozens of bug fixes. Thank you to everyone who tested the staging betas and helped shape this release. Shoutout to **CovOp** and **CePheid** on Discord who contributed heavily to squashing bugs and developing the new Calendar block!

::party-popper:: **New Features**

**The Chapters Page, Reimagined** - One of Penpoint's most-requested features is finally here. The Chapters page has been rebuilt from the ground up. Each chapter now has its own customizable block-based layout, just like Character Sheets, so you can design a chapter view that fits how *you* plan stories. Every scene now stands on its own with its own characters, locations, items, and POV. The new outliner sidebar mirrors Manuscript Mode, so navigating between chapters and scenes feels instantly familiar. Configure your default scene layout in Settings -> Scene Layout. Your existing chapters carry over with their current view intact, nothing to redo.

**Build Your Own World's Calendar** - Your book now has its own calendar system. Pick **Standard (Gregorian)** for real-world stories, **Day Counter** for stories told by day number ("Day 47" instead of "March 12"), or go wild with **Custom** and design a fantasy calendar from scratch: your own months, weekdays, festival periods, leap days, even BCE/CE-style epochs. Every scene and chapter date uses your chosen mode automatically. *This also paves the way for a future rework of the Timeline view, coming in a future update!*

**Customize Your Book Covers** - Your books on the shelf just got a lot more personal. Pick from 12 preset colors or mix your own with a built-in color picker. Layer on a texture like Grain, Linen, Leather, or Parchment for that hardcover feel. Or go all out and upload your own cover image, cropped to fit. If your image already has the title on it, you can hide Penpoint's text overlay so it doesn't compete. All your choices travel with your book through backups and restores.

**Customize Your Character Arc View** - The Character Arc page now has a gear icon in the visualizer header to control what's visible. Choose to show plot points for all characters, just the active one, or none at all. Toggle "Always show labels" off for a clean comparison view; you can still hover or click any keyframe to reveal its label. The active character's line is also a bit thicker now so it stands out from any overlays. **Plus**: you can now assign chapters (and specific scenes) to individual plot points on character arcs, just like story arcs. Open any plot point in edit mode to link chapters, or switch to view mode to see them as a read-only list.

**Search Works in Manuscript Mode** - The Glorious Searchbar popup (Ctrl+K) now works while you're writing, not just in Worldbuilding. Search for a character, location, item, chapter, or anything else. Clicking a result opens it in your Minipen sidebar without taking you away from your manuscript. Scene results jump you straight to that scene in the editor.

**See Where Your Locations Appear** - Every location now shows which chapters it appears in, right on its detail page. Open "The Haunted Library" and see at a glance that it shows up in Chapters 3, 7, and 12. In edit mode, you can assign or remove chapter connections directly from the location itself.

**Full Version History in the App** - Settings -> About now has a "See past versions" button. Click it to browse every version's release notes without leaving Penpoint.

**Version Protection for Your Books** - If you open a book on one computer with a newer version of Penpoint, then try to open it on another running an older version, Penpoint will tell you exactly what's happening and ask you to update before opening the book.

**New "Alice" Theme** - A soft cherry blossom pink theme for those who like their writing space a little more lovely. Find it in Settings, Interface alongside the other themes.

**Reset the Demo Book** - The Sherlock Holmes demo book now has a Reset option in its right-click menu. Restore it to its original state any time you want a clean tour.

::feather:: **Improvements**

**Find Characters by Their Nickname** - Anywhere you add a character to something (a scene, a relationship, an item, a location, a chapter, or an arc), you can now search by their alias instead of their full name. Type "Lizzy" and Elizabeth Bennet shows up.

**Filter Items by Location** - You can now filter your Items list by location. Select one or more locations in the filter sidebar and only items tied to those locations will show.

**Smoother Startup Experience** - If you haven't opened Penpoint in a while, you might have a few things waiting: an update, a data upgrade, maybe an announcement. They now queue up and appear one at a time in a sensible order, so you're never overwhelmed by overlapping pop-ups. The analytics prompt also has a new "Not now" option.

**Refreshed Chapters Tutorial** - The Chapters tutorial has been rewritten to walk you through the new block-based layout, with clearer steps and better example data.

**Tag Viewer for Multi-Scene Chapters** - The Tag Viewer now works properly across chapters with multiple scenes, showing tags at the right level so you can see what's tagged where without confusion.

**Keyboard Navigation in Notebook and Items** - Arrow keys now move to the visually adjacent card instead of jumping alphabetically, and Alt+Left/Alt+Right cleanly goes back or forward without also shifting your highlight. The Items page also picked up Up and Down arrow keys for jumping between rows.

**Every Word Counts** - The community word count in your stats panel now shows every word written in Penpoint since launch, right down to the last one. We just crossed a million, and watching that number climb is a lot more fun when you can see it tick upward.

**Historical / Non-fiction Template** - Renamed the "Historical" New Book template to "Historical / Non-fiction" for better discoverability.

::bug:: **Bug Fixes**

**Chapter Descriptions Preserved Across Upgrades** - Fixed an upgrade path where chapter descriptions could be lost when migrating to the new block-based chapter layout. Every upgrade path now preserves your chapter descriptions completely.

**Character Arc Overlays Lining Up** - When comparing multiple character arcs, plot point markers on background arcs could drift out of alignment with the active character's, even when both arcs had the same number of points. They line up properly now.

**Crosslinks Now Show Scenes** - Items, Locations, and Characters that appear in chapters now drill down to specific scenes, not just the chapter as a whole. Character chapter lists also show the correct scene numbers (instead of internal positions), and Story Arc plot points correctly filter to their assigned scenes in view mode.

**Arc Scene Assignment Clarity** - Assigned scenes in your arc plot points now show a filled square so you can see at a glance what's linked. The Assign and Remove buttons are always visible, no hovering required.

**Right-Click Submenus Staying on Screen** - Fixed several cases where right-click submenus could get cut off at the screen edge, especially near a corner. When a submenu flips to the opposite side to fit, it now sits flush against the parent menu instead of overlapping or leaving a gap.

**Cleaner Scene Names** - Adding a second scene to a chapter no longer leaves you with two rows labeled "Empty Scene". New scenes are now named "Scene 1", "Scene 2", and so on. Scenes you've already named are left alone.

**Duplicate Scenes Under Rapid Clicking** - Fixed a rare race condition where rapid clicks during scene creation could occasionally create duplicate or orphaned scenes. Scene creation is now reliable even under heavy clicking.

**Calendar and Chapter Card Polish** - Several calendar UI improvements and chapter card display fixes, plus better submenu positioning throughout.

**Notebook Rename Fixes** - Fixed two rename issues: renaming a folder in the sidebar could fail if that same folder was visible in the main grid at the same time (the rename box would flash and disappear before you could type), and renaming a note while it was open in the viewer didn't refresh the title inside the editor until you switched notes.

**Export Title Page Remembers Your Book** - The Title field in the manuscript export dialog now always shows your current book's name, not whatever you last exported. If you've customized the title before, Penpoint remembers your custom version per book.

**Location Renames Now Update the List** - Renaming a location in edit mode didn't update the name in the left-hand list until you reloaded the app. Now the list updates on its own right after you stop typing, and the title field feels snappier too.

**Faster Search Results After Editing** - If you fixed a word in your manuscript and then searched for it, the old version could still show up in search results for several seconds. Now results update the moment you open the searchbar.

**Manuscript Cursor Hiding Behind Toolbar** - The typing cursor could scroll up behind the formatting toolbar, letting you type text you couldn't see. Fixed.

**Domain URLs in Manuscripts** - Fixed a rare bug where domain endings like .it could sneak into your manuscript as hidden links. Your text is now safe from sneaky URLs.

**Cutting Text No Longer Reduces Your Word Count** - Using cut and paste to move text around no longer affects your daily word count.

**Word Count Stats After Midnight** - If you were writing past midnight, the "Manuscript size (+X today)" line in your daily stats could show the wrong number. Fixed.

**Manuscript Folders Staying Put** - If you made a few empty folders in Manuscript Mode's chapter outliner back to back and dragged them into order, they could quietly swap positions when you came back later. Now they stay exactly where you put them.

**Notebook Panel Widths Drifting** - If you resized the folder tree or preview panel in the Notebook and then switched to another page and came back, the panels would shift slightly each time. Fixed.

**Back Button Not Clearing Item Selection** - On the Items page, pressing Back right after selecting an item didn't actually deselect it, which threw off the back and forward history. Fixed.

**Search Result Highlights on Hover** - When hovering over a search result, the matched search term could become hard to read. Fixed.

**Closing the App During the Welcome Tour** - The X button now properly closes Penpoint during the first-run welcome tour. Before, you'd have to wait for the tour to finish (or force-quit) to get out.

Thank you for using Penpoint! Happy writing.
